Seven families of victims injured or killed in the Tumbler Ridge school shooting in Canada have filed lawsuits against OpenAI and CEO Sam Altman. The families accuse the company of negligence after OpenAI's systems flagged the suspected shooter's ChatGPT activity involving gun violence conversations but the company decided not to alert police, allegedly to protect its reputation and upcoming IPO. OpenAI only deactivated the account; the shooter simply created a new one following OpenAI's own instructions. The families also claim GPT-4o's 'defective' design played a part in the mass shooting. Altman has apologized, saying he was deeply sorry they did not alert law enforcement.

## 对 AI 行业的影响

这起诉讼将 AI 系统责任边界问题首次推入主流司法视野。OpenAI 在已知威胁信号后保持沉默的核心动机被指向 IPO 时间线，说明商业压力可能在关键时刻压倒安全判断。它可能成为 AI 行业安全责任立法的催化剂。
